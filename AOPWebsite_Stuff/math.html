Proof that (-a)(-b) = ab

Suppose we have a number, R, that takes the form: ab + (-a)b + (-a)(-b)
Using the distributive property, it can be rewritten as: R = (a + -a)b + (-a)(-b)
This simplifies to (0)b + (-a)(-b) or R = (-a)(-b)
We can also rewrite the original equation as: R = ab + -a(b + -b)
Similarly, this simplifies to R = ab
Thus, through the transitive property, (-a)(-b) = ab
